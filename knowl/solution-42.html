Solution 3.4.5.1.

The highest-order term of \(f(x)\) is \(2x^4\text{,}\) so we will factor out \(x^4\text{.}\) The other factor will have each term divided by \(x^4\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} f(x) &= 2x^4 - 3x^2 + 5x - 7\\ &= x^4 \cdot (\frac{2x^4}{x^4} - \frac{3x^2}{x^4} + \frac{5x}{x^4} - \frac{7}{x^4}\\ &= x^4 \cdot (2 - \frac{3}{x^2} + \frac{5}{x^3} - \frac{7}{x^4} \end{align*}

The new formula introduces a discontinuity at \(x=0\text{,}\) but that does not change our limits as \(x \to \pm\infty\text{.}\)

To calculate end behavior, we use limits as \(x \to \pm\infty\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to +\infty} f(x) &= \lim_{x \to +\infty} x^4 \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{x^2} + \frac{5}{x^3} - \frac{7}{x^4}\right)\\ &= (+\infty)^4 \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{(+\infty)^2} + \frac{5}{(+\infty)^3} - \frac{7}{(+\infty)^4}\right)\\ &= +\infty \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{\infty} + \frac{5}{\infty} - \frac{7}{\infty}\right)\\ &= +\infty \cdot (2 - 0 + 0 - 0) = 2 \cdot \infty = \infty \end{align*}

That is, the right end behavior of \(f(x)\) is a branch \(y \to +\infty\) as \(x \to +\infty\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) &= \lim_{x \to -\infty} x^4 \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{x^2} + \frac{5}{x^3} - \frac{7}{x^4}\right)\\ &= (-\infty)^4 \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{(-\infty)^2} + \frac{5}{(-\infty)^3} - \frac{7}{(-\infty)^4}\right)\\ &= +\infty \cdot \left(2 - \frac{3}{\infty} + \frac{5}{-\infty} - \frac{7}{\infty}\right)\\ &= +\infty \cdot (2 - 0 + 0 - 0) = 2 \cdot \infty = \infty \end{align*}

So the left end behavior of \(f(x)\) is another branch with \(y \to +\infty\) as \(x \to -\infty\text{.}\) Both arms of the graph reach upward, consistent with any polynomial having an even dominant power.
