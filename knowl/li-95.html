Item.

\(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3}{2x+5}\)

To evaluate this limit, we would write it in terms of a limit form involving \(-\infty\) for each \(x\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3}{2x+5} &= \frac{3}{2(-\infty)+5}\\ &= \frac{3}{-\infty+5}\\ &= \frac{3}{-\infty}\\ &= 0 \end{align*}

The interpretation of this is that \(g(x) = \frac{3}{2x+5}\) has left end behavior \(y \to 0\) as \(x \to -\infty\text{,}\) meaning there is a horizontal asymptote \(y=0\) on the left.
