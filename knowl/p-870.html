A graph of \(f(x)\) illustrates these behaviors. It has a local minimum at \(x=-1-\sqrt{3}\text{,}\) a local maximum at \(x=-1+\sqrt{3}\text{,}\) and another local minimum at \(x=2\text{.}\) \(f(x)\) also has points of inflection at \(x=\pm \sqrt{2}\text{.}\) In the graph, the extremes are marked with circular points, while the inflection points are marked as squares.
