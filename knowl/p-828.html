The next step is to complete sign analysis. We need to find the factorization. Using either factoring by grouping or technology, we find

\begin{equation*} f'(x) = 2(x^2-2)(2x-3)\text{.} \end{equation*}

The individual factors yield roots at \(x=\pm \sqrt{2}\) and \(x=\frac{3}{2}\text{.}\) (Don't be distracted by the constant factor 2, since no values of \(x\) make \(2=0\text{.}\)) We mark these three points on the number line to determine our test intervals \((-\infty,-\sqrt{2})\text{,}\) \((-\sqrt{2},\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) \((\sqrt{2}, \frac{3}{2})\text{,}\) and \((\frac{3}{2},\infty)\text{.}\) We need to test the sign of \(f'(x)\text{,}\) so we calculate the sign or value of \(f'(x)=2(x^2-2)(2x-3)\) at points in each interval.

  • \(f'(-4)=2((-4)^2-2)(2(-4)-3)=2(14)(-11) \lt 0\) (negative)

  • \(f'(0)=2((0)^2-2)(2(0)-3)=2(-2)(-3) \gt 0\) (positive)

  • \(f'(1.45)=2((1.45)^2-2)(2(1.45)-3)=2(0.1025)(-0.1) \lt 0\) (negative)

  • \(f'(2)=2((2)^2-2)(2(2)-3)=2(2)(1) > 0\) (positive)
