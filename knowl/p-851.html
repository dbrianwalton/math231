The Mean Value Theorem gives us a tool to understand this as well. If \(f(x)\) is differentiable on \(I\text{,}\) then each of the average rates of change will match the value of \(f'(x)\) at points \(\xi_1 \in (x_1,x_2)\) and \(\xi_2 \in (x_2,x_3)\text{.}\) Consequently, if we can show that \(f'(x)\) is increasing, the average rates of change must be increasing. Similarly, knowing that \(f'(x)\) is decreasing means that average rates of change must decrease.
