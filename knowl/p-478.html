Now that we know \(R(x) = \frac{(x-1)(x+2)(x-3)}{(x+2)^2(x-3)}\text{,}\) we look for common factors so that we can interpret the discontinuities. The factors \((x+2)\) and \((x-3)\) are both common factors. After canceling, the simplified formula (which still is naturally undefined at \(x=-2\)) is

\begin{equation*} R(x) = \frac{x-1}{x+2}, \quad x \ne 3\text{.} \end{equation*}

The additional constraint \(x \ne 3\) appears because we are talking about equality of equations.
