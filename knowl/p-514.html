Polynomials are usually written as a sum of terms. Because limit forms adding \(+\infty\) and \(-\infty\) are indeterminate, we need to rewrite polynomials in a new form prior to the limit. Multiplying infinities does not create problems, so our strategy will be to rewrite the sum as a product. We could do a full factorization, but that is more work than is needed. Instead, we factor out the highest power of \(x\text{.}\) The highest-order term's coefficient will be a constant, and all other terms will be their coefficients divided by a power of \(x\text{.}\)
