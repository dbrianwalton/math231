To find the related rates equation, we need to compute the derivative with respect to time. Because the radius function is raised to a power, the derivative will require the chain rule.

\begin{align*} \frac{dV}{dt} = V'(t) &= \frac{d}{dt}\left[ \frac{4}{3} \pi (r(t))^3 \right]\\ &= \frac{4}{3} \pi \frac{d}{dr}[r^3] \cdot \frac{d}{dt}[r(t)]\\ &= \frac{4}{3} \pi \cdot 3 r^2 \cdot \frac{dr}{dt}\\ &= 4 \pi \cdot r^2 \cdot \frac{dr}{dt} \end{align*}

In the chain rule, the derivative of the outside was \(\frac{dV}{dr}\) while the derivative of the inside was \(\frac{dr}{dt}\text{.}\)
