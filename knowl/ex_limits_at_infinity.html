Example 3.4.4.

  • \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} -2x^2 + 4\)

    In the limit, each \(x \to +\infty\text{,}\) so the limit form is \(-2 \cdot (\infty)^2 + 4\text{.}\) We know \(\infty\cdot\infty = \infty\) and \(-2 \cdot \infty = -\infty\) and \(-\infty + 4 = -\infty\text{.}\) We would write:

    \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to \infty} -2x^2 + 4 &= -2(\infty)^2 + 4\\ &= -2(\infty) + 4\\ &= -\infty + 4\\ &= -\infty \end{align*}

    The interpretation of this is that \(f(x) = -2x^2+4\) has right end behavior \(y \to -\infty\) as \(x \to +\infty\text{.}\)

  • \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3}{2x+5}\)

    To evaluate this limit, we would write it in terms of a limit form involving \(-\infty\) for each \(x\text{:}\)

    \begin{align*} \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3}{2x+5} &= \frac{3}{2(-\infty)+5}\\ &= \frac{3}{-\infty+5}\\ &= \frac{3}{-\infty}\\ &= 0 \end{align*}

    The interpretation of this is that \(g(x) = \frac{3}{2x+5}\) has left end behavior \(y \to 0\) as \(x \to -\infty\text{,}\) meaning there is a horizontal asymptote \(y=0\) on the left.
