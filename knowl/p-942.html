Having identified the quantities we know and what we need to find, we need to find an equation that relates the quantities \(A, B, C\text{.}\) Because the triangle is a right triangle, the Pythagorean theorem gives us the equation we need,

\begin{equation*} C^2 = A^2+B^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Notice that we don't need to solve for \(C\text{,}\) if we think of the equation as relating the squared distance.
