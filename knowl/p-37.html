More probably, we want to think about arbitrary points, so we will drop the subscripts and think about the variables \(x\) and \(y\) directly. An equation for the line passing through \((x,y)=(a,b)\) and having a slope \(m\) is given by

\begin{equation*} y - b = m \cdot (x-a)\text{.} \end{equation*}

This equation is called the point-slope equation of a line. We often rewrite the equation by isolating \(y\) as a function of \(x\text{.}\)
