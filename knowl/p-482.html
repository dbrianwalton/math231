For the other limit of interest, the factor \(x-3\) canceled completely from the denominator in the simplification. This means the reduced formula is continuous at \(x=3\text{.}\) The value of the reduced formula will be the limit.

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to 3} R(x) = \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x-1}{x+2} = \frac{3-1}{3+2} = \frac{2}{5}\text{.} \end{equation*}

There is no additional required work.
