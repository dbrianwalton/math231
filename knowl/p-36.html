The definition of slope can be used to create an equation of a line. If we know one point \((x,y)=(a,b)\) (think of \(a\) and \(b\) as placeholders for that knowledge), then for any other point \((x,y)=(x_2,y_2)\text{,}\) we know that

\begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{y_2-b}{x_2-a} = m\text{.} \end{equation*}

Equivalently, this means \(\Delta y = m \cdot \Delta x\) or

\begin{equation*} y_2 - b = m \cdot(x_2 - a)\text{.} \end{equation*}

The subscripts on \(x_2\) and \(y_2\) were meant to indicate we were thinking about some specific point.
