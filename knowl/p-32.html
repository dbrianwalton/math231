But now consider a particular point, say \((x,y)=(2,3)\text{.}\) In the context of that point, we will use \(x=2\) and \(y=3\text{.}\) The expression \(3x+2y\) now has a specific value,

\begin{equation*} 3x+2y = 3(2)+2(3) = 12\text{,} \end{equation*}

so that \(3x+2y=12\) is a true statement. This means that the point \((2,3)\) is in the relation defined by the equation and therefore a point on its graph.
