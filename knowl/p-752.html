The derivatives of the powers will be the same constant multiples times the original power times \(x\) raised to a power decreased by one. The derivative of the linear term \(5x-1\) will be the slope \(5\text{.}\) Putting this together, we write:

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\big[4x^5+3x^4-7x^2+5x-1] &= 4 \cdot 5x^4 + 3 \cdot 4x^3 - 7 \cdot 2x + 5\\ &= 20x^4 +12x^3 - 14x + 5 \end{align*}

That is, the function \(f(x)=4x^5+3x^4-7x^2+5x-1\) has a derivative function \(20x^4+12x^3-14x+5\text{.}\)
