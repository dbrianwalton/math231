There are three different quantities being discussed: the average annual wage \(w\text{,}\) the number of employees\(n\text{,}\) and the total annual wages for the company \(T\text{.}\) The question of the problem is to find \(\frac{dT}{dt}\text{.}\) We start with values \(n=800\) and \(w=45000\text{.}\) Because the company's work force is shrinking, we have a negative rate of change \(\frac{dn}{dt} = -50\) per year. The annual wage is increasing by a percentage rate, so we need to multiply the current wage rate by 10% to find

\begin{equation*} \frac{dw}{dt} = 0.10 \cdot 45000 = 4500 \, \text{dollars}/\text{year}\text{.} \end{equation*}
