The relation between these variables is given by the equation

\begin{equation*} T = n \cdot w\text{.} \end{equation*}

The related rates equation is found by differentiating, remembering that each of the variables is actually a function of time \(t\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{dT}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}[n \cdot w]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Because the formula has a product, we must use the product rule.

\begin{equation*} \frac{dT}{dt} = \frac{dn}{dt} \cdot w + n \cdot \frac{dw}{dt}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Using the given values, we find

\begin{align*} \frac{dT}{dt} &= (-50 / \text{year}) \cdot (45000 \, \text{dollars}) + (800) \cdot (4500 \, \text{dollars}/\text{year})\\ &= -2250000 \, \text{dollars}/\text{year} + 3600000 \, \text{dollars}/\text{year}\\ &= 1350000 \, \text{dollars}/\text{year} \end{align*}

Because the calculated rate of change is positive, the total wage costs for the company are increasing.
