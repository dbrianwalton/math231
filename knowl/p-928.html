Many physical quantities are related to one another and equations are used to define these relationships. A sphere has many different measurable attributes, such as the volume, the surface area, the diameter, and the radius. Equations relate the measurements, such as relating volume and radius by \(V=\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3\) or relating surface area and radius by \(A = 4 \pi r^2\text{.}\)
