The inequalities that define the properties of increasing and decreasing are related to the calculation of an average rate of change. For \(a \lt b\text{,}\) the inequality \(f(b) \gt f(a)\) is equivalent to \(f(b) - f(a) \gt 0\text{,}\) which is in turn equivalent to a positive average rate of change \(\displaystyle \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \gt 0\text{.}\) Similarly, \(f(b) \lt f(a)\) is equivalent to having a negative average rate of change \(\displaystyle \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \lt 0\text{.}\)
