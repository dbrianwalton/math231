Functions can have widely varying behaviors. One of the most fundamental properties of a function is continuity, which is often framed in terms of connectedness. When a function has a break or a jump, it is not continuous or discontinuous. However, mathematicians realized that there is more power in the concept by thinking about continuity as saying that a value of a function is well-approximated by nearby values in the domain. This resulted in the development of the limit of a function, which is at the heart of all ideas in calculus.
