As you follow this example, think about what the last operation is at each step and what inverse operation will eliminate it.

\begin{align*} \frac{2}{3x-1}+4 &= 8\\ \frac{2}{3x-1} &= 8+-4 = 4 \end{align*}

The fraction (quotient) is isolated, so we will apply the reciprocal to both sides, reversing what is divided:

\begin{align*} \frac{2}{3x-1} &= 4\\ \div \frac{2}{3x-1} &= \div 4\\ \frac{3x-1}{2} &= \frac{1}{4} \end{align*}

We can now proceed with isolation as before:

\begin{align*} \frac{3x-1}{2} &= \frac{1}{4}\\ 3x-1 &= 2 \cdot \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2}\\ 3x &= \frac{1}{2} + 1 = \frac{3}{2}\\ x &= \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \end{align*}

Thus, our solution is \(x = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
