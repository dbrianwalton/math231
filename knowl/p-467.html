Infinite limits exactly correspond to a vertical asymptote. In FigureÂ 3.3.1, both \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) have a vertical asymptote at \(x=-2\) because the factor \(x+2\) in the denominator did not cancel away. From the graph, we see the left segment of the graph extends to the bottom of the graph, corresponding to

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to -2^-} g(x) = -\infty\text{.} \end{equation*}

On the right segment, the graph extends to the top of the graph, corresponding to

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to -2^+} g(x) = +\infty\text{.} \end{equation*}
