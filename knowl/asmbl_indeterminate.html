Indeterminate Limit Forms.

Limit forms that are indeterminate can not be calculated from the limits of the individual terms but require rewriting the original formula into a new form. Indeterminate limit forms include limits where it looks like infinitely large or infinitely small things cancel each other:

  • \(\infty - \infty\text{:}\) If \(f(x) \to \infty\) and \(g(x) \to \infty\text{,}\) then \(f(x)-g(x)\) needs to be rewritten to find its limit.

  • \(0 \cdot \infty\text{:}\) If \(f(x) \to 0\) and \(g(x) \to \infty\text{,}\) then \(f(x) \cdot g(x)\) needs to be rewritten to find its limit.

  • \(\frac{0}{0}\text{:}\) If \(f(x) \to 0\) and \(g(x) \to 0\text{,}\) then \(f(x) \div g(x)\) needs to be rewritten to find its limit.

  • \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\text{:}\) If \(f(x) \to \infty\) and \(g(x) \to \infty\text{,}\) then \(f(x) \div g(x)\) needs to be rewritten to find its limit.
