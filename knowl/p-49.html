Now that we have our model equation, we can substitute the new value of \(D = 12.8 \, \text{cm}\) into our equation,

\begin{equation*} M = 38 + 9 \cdot (12.8 - 12)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Can you see how \(\Delta D = 0.8 \, \text{cm}\) is the increment of change of the diameter and how that will lead to \(\Delta M = 9 \cdot 0.8 = 7.2 \, \text{kg}\text{?}\) Consequently, our model predicts \(M = 38 + 7.2 = 45.2 \, \text{kg}\text{.}\) We predict the biomass of the tree will be 45.2 kg.
