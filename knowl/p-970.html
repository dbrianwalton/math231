In the case that \(f''(a) \gt 0\text{,}\) we find that \(f'(x)\) will be negative on an interval \((a-\delta, a)\) and \(positive\) on an interval \((a,a+\delta)\text{.}\) By the first derivative test of monotonicity, this implies \(f(x)\) has a local minimum at \(x=a\text{.}\) In a similar way, when \(f''(a) \lt 0\text{,}\) we find that \(f'(x)\) will be positive on an interval \((a-\delta, a)\) and \(negative\) on an interval \((a,a+\delta)\) so that \(f(x)\) has a local minimum at \(x=a\text{.}\)
