Our first step is to rewrite the original function so that the process of differentiation of generalized polynomials applies (first three lines). Then we can apply the power rule to each term.

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5\sqrt{x} (x^2 + 2x - 5)\big] & = \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5 x^{1/2}(x^2 + 2x - 5) \big]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5x^{5/2} + 10 x^{3/2} - 25 x^{1/2} \big]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5x^{5/2} + 10 x^{3/2} - 25 x^{1/2} \big]\\ &= 5 \cdot \frac{5}{2}x^{3/2} + 10 \cdot \frac{3}{2} x^{1/2} - 25 \cdot \frac{1}{2} x^{-1/2}\\ &= \frac{25}{2}x^{3/2} + 15 x^{1/2} - \frac{25}{2} x^{-1/2} \end{align*}
