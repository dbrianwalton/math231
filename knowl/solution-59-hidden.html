The first example looks at a function that is originally written as a quotient. Because the denominator is a simple power, we can rewrite this in the form of a generalized polynomial.

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{2x^5 - x^3 + 7x - 5}{4x^2} \right] &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{2x^5}{4x^2} - \frac{x^3}{4x^2} + \frac{7x}{4x^2} - \frac{5}{4x^2} \right]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{2} x^3 - \frac{1}{4} x + \frac{7}{4} x^{-1} - \frac{5}{4} x^{-2} \right]\\ &= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3x^2 - \frac{1}{4} + \frac{7}{4} \cdot -1x^{-2} - \frac{5}{4} \cdot -2x^{-3}\\ &= \frac{3}{2} x^2 - \frac{1}{4} - \frac{7}{4x^2} + \frac{5}{2x^3} \end{align*}

Note that for negative powers, when the power drops by 1, it moves away from zero. A common mistake is to reduce the size of the power rather than literally subtracting 1, which is what is required.

The second example looks at a function that is originally written as a product. In addition, the function involves a square root. To proceed with this problem, we need to recognize that a square root corresponds to a power of \(\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) In addition, we need to multiply out the terms in order to have a formula in the standard representation.

Our first step is to rewrite the original function so that the process of differentiation of generalized polynomials applies (first three lines). Then we can apply the power rule to each term.

\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5\sqrt{x} (x^2 + 2x - 5)\big] & = \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5 x^{1/2}(x^2 + 2x - 5) \big]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5x^{5/2} + 10 x^{3/2} - 25 x^{1/2} \big]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\big[ 5x^{5/2} + 10 x^{3/2} - 25 x^{1/2} \big]\\ &= 5 \cdot \frac{5}{2}x^{3/2} + 10 \cdot \frac{3}{2} x^{1/2} - 25 \cdot \frac{1}{2} x^{-1/2}\\ &= \frac{25}{2}x^{3/2} + 15 x^{1/2} - \frac{25}{2} x^{-1/2} \end{align*}
