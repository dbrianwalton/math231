For a line, the increments of change are proportional to one another. Equivalently, a line has the same average rate of change between any two points. That is, there is a constant, usually designated by \(m\text{,}\) such that for any two points on the line, \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) the increments of change are related by

\begin{equation*} \Delta y = m \cdot \Delta x \qquad \Leftrightarrow \qquad \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = m\text{.} \end{equation*}

For a line, this constant rate of change is called the slope of the line.
