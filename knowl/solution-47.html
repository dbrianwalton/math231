Solution 4.1.4.1.

Start by marking the point on the graph at \(x=-2\text{.}\) The point approximately looks like \((-2,-2.7)\text{.}\) We draw a tangent line through this point to match the slope on a very small interval here. You should get a graph that looks something like the following.

We look for another point on the line that might be easy to estimate, and discover that the line passes through \((1,0)\text{.}\) Using these two points from the tangent line, we can calculate the approximate slope of the tangent line, which is the derivative at \(x=-2\) of the original graph.

\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dx}\Big|_{-2} &\approx \frac{0--2.7}{1--2}\\ &= \frac{2.7}{3} = 0.9 \end{align*}

Note again that because we are estimating points visually based on grid lines, these calculations are only estimates. Any reasonable values based on reasonable approximations of the tangent line will be acceptable.
