Using the definition of the derivative and the statement \(f'(a)=0\text{,}\) we know

\begin{align*} f''(a) &= \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)-f'(a)}{x-a}\\ &= \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{x-a} \end{align*}

For \(x\) close enough to \(a\text{,}\) the sign of \(f''(a)\) must be the same as the sign of \(\frac{f'(x)}{x-a}\text{.}\) Consequently, the sign of \(f'(x)\) must match the sign of \(f''(a) \cdot (x-a)\text{.}\)
