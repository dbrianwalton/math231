The overall function is a difference of two formulas, \(2x f(x^2)\) and \(\frac{1}{g^3(x)}\text{.}\) The first of these is a product of \(2x\) and \(f(x^2)\text{.}\) We know how to find the derivative of \(2x\) (a linear function) and \(f(x^2)\) is a composition of \(f\) with an inner function \(x^2\text{.}\) The second term can be rewritten as \((g(x))^-3\text{,}\) which is itself now a composition of \(u^{-3}\) with \(u=g(x)\text{.}\)
