We previously found

\begin{gather*} \lim_{x \to -2^-} g(x) = -1\\ \lim_{x \to -2^+} g(x) = 3 \end{gather*}

from which we decided that \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -2} g(x)\) does not exist. Furthermore, \(g(-2)\) itself also does not exist since \(x=-2\) is not in any of the restricted domains. Since both values fail to exist, they can not be equal. \(g\) is not continuous at \(x=-2\text{.}\)
