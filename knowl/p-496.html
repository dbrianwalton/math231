The other end-behavior we will look at is unbounded growth. Think about the graph of a parabola, \(y=x^2\text{.}\) It forms the shape of a U with the two arms reaching up forever. This idea of reaching up is what we want to understand. As limits, we say \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} x^2 = +\infty\) (left arm reaches up) and \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to +\infty} x^2 = +\infty\) (right arm reaches up). A cubic has one arm reaching up and one reaching down, represented by limits

\begin{gather*} \lim_{x \to -\infty} x^3 = -\infty\\ \lim_{x \to +\infty} x^3 = +\infty \end{gather*}
