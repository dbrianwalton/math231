What variables are involved? The water filling the cup forms the shape of a circular cone that fits inside of the cup. Consequently, the radii and heights of the cup and the water cone form similar triangles. Let \(r\) be the radius of the cone of water at the surface and let \(h\) be the height or depth of water in the cup. By similarity, we require the ratios \(r/4\) and \(h/10\) be equal, which results in an equation

\begin{equation*} \frac{r}{4} = \frac{h}{10} \qquad \Leftrightarrow \qquad 10r = 4h\text{.} \end{equation*}
