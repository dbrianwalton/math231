To understand the concept of rate of change, we need to consider the relationship between two different points, \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) from the same graph. If our variables are \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) then we might represent one point as having coordinates \(A=(x_1,y_1)\) and the second point as having coordinates \(B=(x_2,y_2)\text{.}\) We calculate how the variables change going from \(A\) to \(B\text{:}\)

\begin{gather*} \Delta x = x_2 - x_1\\ \Delta y = y_2 - y_1 \end{gather*}

Note that we think of \(\Delta x\) as the name of a single quantity. The order of subtraction is chosen so that we can legitimately say \(x_2 = x_1 + \Delta x\) and \(y_2 = y_1 + \Delta y\text{.}\) We call \(\Delta x\) the increment of change in \(x\) and \(\Delta y\) the increment of change in \(y\) as we go from \(A\) to \(B\text{.}\)
