“\(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = +\infty\)” means that the values \(f(x)\) get larger and larger (positively) as \(x \to a^+\text{.}\) More specifically, no matter what number \(M \gt 0\) you choose, as \(x \to a^+\text{,}\) we eventually will have \(f(x) \gt M\text{.}\) (Close enough to \(x=a\) with \(x \gt a\text{,}\) \(f(x)\) will stay above \(y=M\text{.}\))
