Now that we have the related rates equation, we use the known values to solve for the unknown value. We know at the instant in question \(r=0.8\) mm and \(\frac{dr}{dt}=-0.05\) mm/s. We substitute these values into the equation to get

\begin{equation*} \frac{dV}{dt} = 4 \pi (0.8 \, \text{mm})^2 \cdot (-0.05 \, \text{mm/s}) = -0.128 \pi \, \text{mm}^3/\text{s} \approx -0.4021 \, \text{mm}^3/\text{s}\text{.} \end{equation*}

A negative rate of change occurs because the volume is also decreasing as time passes.
