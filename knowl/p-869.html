We use \(f'(x)\) to understand where \(f(x)\) is increasing or decreasing, and \(f''(x)\) to understand where \(f(x)\) is concave up or concave down. We start by calculating the derivatives so that we can do sign analysis.

\begin{align*} f(x) &= x^4-12x^2+16x\\ f'(x) &= 4x^3-24x+16\\ f''(x) &= 12x^2-24 \end{align*}

To do sign analysis, we need to find the zeros of the functions. If the functions factor, that is the easiest way to accomplish that.

\begin{align*} f(x) &= x(x+4)(x-2)^2\\ f'(x) &= 4(x-2)(x^2+2x-2)\\ f''(x) &= 12(x^2-2) \end{align*}
