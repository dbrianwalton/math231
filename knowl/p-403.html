In FigureÂ 3.1.1, the limit on the left and the limit on the right had different values. This happens whenever the segments lead to different points. We say that the two-sided limit \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -1} f(x)\) does not exist. This is two-sided because the limit does not specify \(x \lt -1\) or \(x \gt -1\text{.}\) When segments on both sides of a point approach the same point, whether it is included or not, the two-sided limit would equal that common value.
