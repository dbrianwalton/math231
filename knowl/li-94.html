Item.

\(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} -2x^2 + 4\)

In the limit, each \(x \to +\infty\text{,}\) so the limit form is \(-2 \cdot (\infty)^2 + 4\text{.}\) We know \(\infty\cdot\infty = \infty\) and \(-2 \cdot \infty = -\infty\) and \(-\infty + 4 = -\infty\text{.}\) We would write:

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to \infty} -2x^2 + 4 &= -2(\infty)^2 + 4\\ &= -2(\infty) + 4\\ &= -\infty + 4\\ &= -\infty \end{align*}

The interpretation of this is that \(f(x) = -2x^2+4\) has right end behavior \(y \to -\infty\) as \(x \to +\infty\text{.}\)
