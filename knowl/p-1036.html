Calculating \(T''(a)\text{,}\) it will be easier to use the product rule than the quotient rule.

\begin{align*} T''(a) &= \frac{d}{da}\left[ \frac{1}{c} \cdot \left( \frac{4}{3} a (a^2+2.25)^{-1/2} - (2-a)((2-a)^2+1)^{-1/2} \right)\right]\\ &= \frac{1}{c}\Big( \frac{4}{3} (1) (a^2+2.25)^{-1/2} \\ & \qquad \qquad + \frac{4}{3} a \cdot {\textstyle \frac{-1}{2}} (a^2+2.25)^{-3/2} \cdot 2a \\ & \qquad \qquad - (-1)((2-a)^2+1)^{-1/2}\\ & \qquad \qquad - (2-a) \cdot {\textstyle \frac{-1}{2}} ((2-a)^2+1)^{-3/2} \cdot 2(2-a) \cdot (-1)\Big)\\ &= \frac{1}{c}\Big( \frac{4}{3} (a^2+2.25)^{-1/2}\\ &\qquad \qquad - \frac{4}{3} a^2 \cdot(a^2+2.25)^{-3/2}\\ & \qquad \qquad + ((2-a)^2+1)^{-1/2}\\ & \qquad \qquad - (2-a)^2 ((2-a)^2+1)^{-3/2} \Big)\\ &= \frac{1}{c}\Big( \frac{4}{3} \frac{a^2+2.25 - a^2}{(a^2+2.25)^{3/2}}\\ & \qquad \qquad + \frac{(2-a)^2+1 - (2-a)^2}{((2-a)^2+1)^{3/2}} \Big)\\ &= \frac{1}{c}\Big( \frac{4}{3} \frac{2.25}{(a^2+2.25)^{3/2}} + \frac{1}{((2-a)^2+1)^{3/2}} \Big) \end{align*}

Because all of the terms are strictly positive, we know \(T''(a) \gt 0\) so that \(T'(a)\) is an increasing function. Consequently, \(T'(a)\) goes from negative to positive, implying that \(T(a)\) has a minimum at \(a \approx 0.962\text{,}\) which will be a global minimum.
