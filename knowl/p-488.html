When we talk about intervals with two numbers as end points, such as \((a,b)\text{,}\) we are working with a bounded interval. When an interval only has one end point, such as \((-\infty,a)\) or \((b,\infty)\text{,}\) we are working with an unbounded interval. Our discussion about limits thus far have focused on limits where the variable approaches some specific value, \(x \to a\text{.}\) These limits deal with bounded subsets of the domain. End behavior deals with limits of a function over unbounded domains, thinking of \(x \to -\infty\) or \(x \to +\infty\text{.}\)
