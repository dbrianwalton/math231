We start by translating the question into an inequality,

\begin{equation*} 4 \lt 3x-5 \lt 5\text{.} \end{equation*}

Technically, this is a compound inequality that \(3x-5 \gt 4\) and \(3x-5 \lt 5\text{.}\) Normally this would require solving the simpler inequalities individually and then finding the intersection (because and means intersect) of the solutions.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c c} 3x-5 \gt 4 & 3x-5 \lt 5 \\ 3x \gt 4+5 & 3x \lt 5+5 \\ 3x \gt 9 & 3x \lt 10 \\ x \gt 3 & x \lt \frac{10}{3} \end{array} \end{equation*}

The first inequality's solution set is \((3,\infty)\text{.}\) The second inequality's solution set is \((-\infty, \frac{10}{3})\text{.}\) The intersection of these sets is \((3, \frac{10}{3})\text{.}\)
