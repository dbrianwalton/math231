If \(f'(x)\) itself is differentiable, then sign analysis of \(f''(x)\) can be used to find intervals of monotonicity of \(f'(x)\text{,}\) which in turn are intervals of concavity for \(f(x)\text{.}\) That is, sign analysis of \(f''(x)\) determines the concavity of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
