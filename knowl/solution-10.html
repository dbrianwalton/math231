Solution 1.3.3.1.

As we work through the operations to isolate the square, we need to remember that the order of operations have powers prioritized before multiplication and addition. Our goal will be to isolate the squared value and then apply the square root to both sides.

\begin{align*} 2x^2 - 1 &= 5\\ 2x^2 &= 5 + 1 = 6\\ x^2 &= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 6 = 3 \end{align*}

At this point, using the square root will give two roots: \(\pm \sqrt{3}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} x^2 & = 3\\ x & = \pm \sqrt{3} \end{align*}

Our equation has two solutions, \(x=+\sqrt{3}\) and \(x=-\sqrt{3}\text{.}\)
