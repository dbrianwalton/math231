We can therefore find the location of the hole in FigureÂ 3.3.1. The \(x\)-value is at \(x=1\text{.}\) The \(y\)-value is found from the limit, something that can be calculated by replacing the discontinuous formula with the simplified and continuous formula:

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x+3)(x-1)}{(x+2)(x-1)} = \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x+3}{x+2} = \frac{1+3}{1+2} = \frac{4}{3}\text{.} \end{equation*}

That is, the hole appears at \((1,\frac{4}{3})\text{.}\)
