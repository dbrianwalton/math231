The boundaries of our solution set exactly correspond to where the graph \(y=3x-5\) intersects the threshold values \(y=4\) and \(y=5\text{.}\) Finding the intersection points corresponds to solving equations \(3x-5=4\) and \(3x-5=5\text{.}\) Once we know those points, we can just check each of the intervals using these end-points to find our solution set. This is what motivates the strategy of sign analysis to solve inequalities.
