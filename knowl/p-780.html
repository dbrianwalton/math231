When we use the derivative rules for a difference, we just subtract the derivatives of the terms separately. The product will require the product rule. We work through the rules in turn to find the final answer.

\begin{align*} h'(x) &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2x f(x^2) - \frac{1}{g^3(x)} \right]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2x f(x^2) \right] - \frac{d}{dx}\left[\left(g(x)\right)^{-3} \right]\\ &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2x \right] \cdot f(x^2) + 2x \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[ f(x^2) \right] - \frac{d}{dx}\left[\left(g(x)\right)^{-3} \right]\\ &= 2 \cdot f(x^2) + 2x \cdot f'(x^2) \cdot (2x) - \frac{d}{dx}\left[\left(g(x)\right)^{-3} \right]\\ &= 2 \cdot f(x^2) + 2x \cdot f'(x^2) \cdot (2x) - (-3u^{-4}) \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \Big|_{u=g(x)}\\ &= 2 \cdot f(x^2) + 4x^2 \cdot f'(x^2) + 3(g(x))^{-4} \cdot g'(x)\\ &= 2 \cdot f(x^2) + 4x^2 \cdot f'(x^2) + \frac{3g'(x)}{(g(x))^{4}} \end{align*}
