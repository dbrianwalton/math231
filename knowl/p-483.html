Limits describe function behavior as the input approaches some value. A limit at a point, say \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) is a \(y\)-value \(y=L\) that the function value \(f(x)\) approaches for sequences of \(x\)-values approaching \(x=a\text{.}\) We also think of a vertical asymptote at \(x=a\) in terms of limits. This occurs when the values of a function \(f(x)\) get larger and larger in size as \(x \to a\text{,}\) and we say \(y \to \pm \infty\text{.}\)
