In basic algebraic formulas, the only way that a formula is undefined (see topic of domain in SubsectionÂ 2.2.1) is if we divide by zero or if we try to take an even root of a negative value. Limits help us understand what happens at values that are not in the domain of an algebraic expression. When a limit exists, it means the expression has a removable discontinuity and the graph has a corresponding hole. Sometimes, the one-sided limits will be infinite, corresponding to a graph that has a vertical asymptote. We will focus on formulas that can be factored and their associated limits.
