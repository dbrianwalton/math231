A function \(f(x)\) is concave up on an interval \(I\) if for any three points \(x_1,x_2,x_3 \in I\) with \(x_1 \lt x_2 \lt x_3\text{,}\) the average rate of change is increasing:

\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x_2)-f(x_1)}{x_2-x_1} \lt \frac{f(x_3)-f(x_2)}{x_3-x_2} \end{equation*}
