The methods of isolation and factoring are the preferred methods because they are more generalizable to other problems and tend to result in simplified answers. The method illustrated for factoring a quadratic might break down if we can not find factors \(p,q\) so that \(pq=ac\) and \(p+q=b\text{.}\) In that case, we can use the method of the quadratic formula.
